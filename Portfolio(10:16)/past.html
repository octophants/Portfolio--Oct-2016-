    Why drips?
    It would have been very easy for me to craft a clean, slick website fit for a quintessential Brooklyn based designer, but I like creating things that are visually striking and have a touch of my own personality. My aesthetics may not always be ‘beautiful’, but I like to think that they stand out enough to make my work memorable. I come up with a lot of crazy concepts in my spare time, and these drippy drips happen to be one of those things.

    Past
    My previous portfolio site was very different from this one, and I received a variety of reactions to it. I had a blast making it, because nothing is more satisfying than evolving squiggly boxes in your sketchbook into functional websites. It's a bit janky in places and I'd be lying if I said I didn't cringe at parts of it, but it has a touch of personality which is endearing in a sense. I made this site when I started realizing my deep hatred for boring 'image-grid on white background portfolios'. It hosts some of my older projects from back when I was still a student – these include a scarily complex infographic, a published design book, an iPad game and some weird illustrations.
